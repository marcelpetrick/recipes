# German Pork Goulash  
## (Schweinegulasch im Schmortopf)

### Ingredients (serves 4)
- 1 kg pork shoulder or pork neck, cut into 3 cm cubes  
- 3 large onions, chopped  
- 2 garlic cloves, minced  
- 2 tbsp sweet paprika  
- (Optional) 1 tsp hot paprika or a pinch of chili  
- 2 tbsp tomato paste  
- 500 ml meat broth (pork or beef)  
- (Optional) 250 ml dry red wine  
- 2 tbsp cooking oil or lard  
- Salt & pepper  
- 1–2 bay leaves  
- 1 tsp caraway seeds or marjoram (optional)  
- (Optional) 1 tbsp flour or sauce thickener  

### Equipment
- Heavy braising pot (Schmortopf)

---

## Instructions

1. **Prepare the meat**  
   Pat pork cubes dry; season with salt & pepper.

2. **Heat the pot**  
   Add oil or lard to your schmortopf over medium-high heat.

3. **Brown the pork**  
   Add meat in batches and brown well. Remove and set aside.

4. **Cook onions & garlic**  
   Add onions to the pot and sauté until lightly browned.  
   Add garlic for 30 seconds.

5. **Season**  
   Return meat to the pot.  
   Add paprika and stir quickly (don’t let it burn).  
   Add tomato paste and fry briefly.

6. **Deglaze & add liquids**  
   Add red wine (if using) and reduce slightly.  
   Pour in broth, add bay leaves and caraway/marjoram.

7. **Slow cook**  
   Cover and simmer on low for **about 60 minutes** until tender.

8. **Thicken (optional)**  
   Add flour slurry or thickener and simmer 5–10 minutes uncovered.

9. **Final seasoning**  
   Remove bay leaves; adjust salt & pepper.

10. **Serve**  
   Pairs well with potatoes, spaetzle, bread dumplings, or noodles.

---

### Tips
- Pork shoulder or neck works best for tenderness and rich flavor.  
- Onions:meat ratio of ~1:1 is traditional in many German goulash styles.  
- Browning the onions more adds deeper flavour.  
- Even better the next day!
